Common Heart Disease Symptoms

Recognizing the warning signs that could indicate heart problems

Emergency Warning

If you are experiencing chest pain, shortness of breath, or symptoms of a heart attack, call emergency services (911) immediately. Don't wait to see if symptoms go away.

Heart disease often develops slowly over time, and many people don't know they have it until they experience a heart attack or heart failure. Recognizing the symptoms early can lead to faster treatment and better outcomes.

Common Symptoms of Heart Disease

Chest Pain or Discomfort

The most common symptom is chest pain (angina) that feels like pressure, squeezing, fullness, or pain in the center or left side of the chest. It may come and go.

Shortness of Breath

Feeling breathless during normal activities or at rest, especially when lying down. This can occur alone or with chest discomfort.

Fatigue & Weakness

Unusual fatigue, weakness, or unexplained tiredness, especially during activities that used to be easy.

Lightheadedness

Feeling dizzy, lightheaded, or fainting. This can be due to insufficient blood flow to the brain.

Irregular Heartbeat

Heart palpitations or feeling like your heart is skipping beats, beating too fast, or pounding.

Swelling

Swelling in the legs, ankles, feet, or abdomen due to fluid buildup. This can indicate heart failure.

Cold Sweats

Breaking out in a cold sweat for no obvious reason, especially if accompanied by other symptoms.

Pain in Upper Body

Pain or discomfort that spreads to the shoulders, arms, back, neck, jaw, or upper stomach.

Symptoms May Differ in Women

Women are more likely than men to experience symptoms other than chest pain when having a heart attack. These symptoms can include:

Nausea and Vomiting

Women are more likely to experience nausea or vomiting during a heart attack.

Back or Jaw Pain

Pain in the jaw, back, or neck is more common in women during a heart attack.

Extreme Fatigue

Unusual fatigue that lasts for several days or sudden severe fatigue for no apparent reason.

Sleep Disturbances

Difficulty sleeping may occur weeks before a heart attack in women.

When to See a Doctor

Contact your doctor if you experience any of the following:

  • Chest pain, discomfort, pressure, or squeezing, even if it goes away
  • Shortness of breath, especially with minimal exertion
  • Unexplained fatigue or weakness
  • Swelling in your feet, ankles, legs, or abdomen
  • Heart palpitations or irregular heartbeat
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
Call 911 immediately if:
  • You experience chest pain or discomfort that lasts more than a few minutes or goes away and comes back
  • You have severe shortness of breath
  • You experience severe pain spreading to the arms, neck, jaw, or back
  • You feel a sense of impending doom, cold sweat, nausea, or lightheadedness
